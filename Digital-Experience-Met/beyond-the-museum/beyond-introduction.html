Introduction

Museums are more than their physical buildings. They have the power and ability to reach beyond their walls and extend their exhibitions, content, and values to those who wish to experience them from afar.

The Metropolitan Museum of Art (Met) uses its website as the main source for collection, exhibition, and learning content. At the same time though, their Google Arts and Culture page has incredible options for individuals who cannot physically visit the museum.

The following two sections will consist of a description and an analysis of what digital content can be found on the Met’s website

I will examine this content from three perspectives:

additional exhibition content           learning           accessibility

in relation to their respective target audiences, visitors post-exhibition, students/teachers, and individuals who cannot physically access the museum building.
