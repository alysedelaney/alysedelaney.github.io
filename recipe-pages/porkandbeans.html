Pork & Beans

serves 2
up to 3 hrs for the beans, 20 minutes for the pork

My boyfriend and I have been making these beans on cold, rainy days when we’re hunkered down inside and have the time to nurse a pot on the stove for an afternoon. I recently learned (via Carla Lalli Music) about the magic of salting your beans while they soak. There’s a popular myth that if you salt beans before they’re finished cooking they’ll never become tender. However, if you salt them while they soak they are so much better seasoned than if you had only salted them at the end, and (shocker) they are still deliciously creamy on the inside.

No worries if you forget to soak your beans overnight- throwing them in some water in the morning and letting them sit for a few hours is usually good enough to get the ball rolling. If you’re buying fresh dried beans (my absolute favorite are Rancho Gordo) you might not even need to soak at all! We like serving our beans with some zippy fresh salsa on top, and a perfectly seared pork chop drizzled in a simple pan sauce.

Ingredients

  • The Veggies:
    • 1 bunch kale, roughly chopped
    • 1 16oz can chickpeas
    • 1 medium sweet potato
    • 1 shallots, or 1 small red onion
    • 2 garlic cloves
    • 1 tbsp garam masala
    • 1 tsp turmeric
    • 1 cup chicken or vegetable stock
    • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • The Topping:
    • 1/2 cup sunflower seeds
    • 2 tbps butter
    • 1/2 tsp garam masala
  • For Serving:
    • 2 cups cooked quinoa, or preferred grain
    • Lemon wedges
